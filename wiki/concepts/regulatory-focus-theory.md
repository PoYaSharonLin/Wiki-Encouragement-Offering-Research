# Regulatory Focus Theory

## Definition

Proposed by Higgins (1998), describes two motivational orientations individuals use when pursuing goals:

**Promotion Focus**
- Guided by ideal self-guides (hopes, aspirations, ideals)
- Strategy: approach states that match the goal (approach)
- Characteristics: faster action, accepts some error risk, stronger response to self-efficacy appeals

**Prevention Focus**
- Guided by ought self-guides (duties, responsibilities, obligations)
- Strategy: avoid states that deviate from the goal (avoidance)
- Characteristics: precise action, aims for zero errors, stronger response to response-efficacy appeals

## Regulatory Fit

When the message framing aligns with an individual's regulatory focus orientation, a "feeling right" effect emerges, significantly increasing persuasion and behavioral intention.

- Keller (2006): Promotion focus × Self-efficacy → highest sunscreen intention (M = 5.00 vs. 2.75)
- Cesario et al. (2004): The sense of fit translates into persuasion through processing fluency

## Manipulation Methods

Based on Keller (2006) Experiment 2 and Freitas & Higgins (2002) standard procedure:
- **Promotion induction**: "Please think about your hopes and aspirations" → manipulation check mean = 4.47
- **Prevention induction**: "Please think about your duties and obligations" → manipulation check mean = 2.98
- Manipulation check: "What was more important for you to do? (1 = ought to, 7 = want to)"

## Contrast with Fear Appeal

| Dimension | Fear Appeal (Prevention) | Encouragement Offering (Promotion) |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Message framing | Emphasizes negative consequences and threats | Emphasizes positive outcomes and growth |
| Motivational mechanism | Threat Appraisal | Self-determination / Growth |
| Applicable context | One-time behaviors > repeated behaviors | Lifestyle behaviors (e.g., diet) |
| Potential risk | Induces anxiety, may backfire | Promotion focus may sacrifice accuracy (mitigated by RAM) |

## Role in the Research

As the **core theoretical foundation** of this research, RFT provides the theoretical basis for replacing Fear Appeal with a "positive message framing":
1. Uses Promotion-focus messages (emphasizing self-efficacy) as the intervention condition
2. Uses Prevention-focus as the control intervention (analogous to the motivational mechanism of Fear Appeal)
3. Predicts via Regulatory Fit theory: Promotion × Self-efficacy message is most effective
