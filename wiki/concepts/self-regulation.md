# Self-Regulation

## Definition

Drawn from Self-Determination Theory (SDT, Deci & Ryan, 1985), describes the continuum of individuals' behavioral motivation:

| Motivation Type | Description | Autonomy |
|-----------------|-------------|----------|
| Amotivation | Does not perceive a connection between behavior and outcome | Lowest |
| External Regulation | Controlled by external rewards and punishments | ↑ |
| Introjected Regulation | Internalizes external pressure (guilt, self-esteem) | ↑ |
| Identified Regulation | Behavior is aligned with personal values | ↑ |
| Integrated Regulation | Behavior is incorporated into self-identity | ↑ |
| Intrinsic Motivation | Driven by pure interest and satisfaction | Highest |

## Role in the Research

Serves as a **control variable (Treatment Self-Regulation)** to rule out the confounding effect that participants with inherently high autonomous motivation may report health data more honestly.

Example items (Pelletier et al., 1997):
- "Because I personally believe it is the best thing for my health" (Identified)
- "Because I would feel ashamed if I did not" (Introjected)
- "Because others would be angry with me if I did not" (External)

## Relationship Between SDT and Regulatory Focus Theory (2026-02-10)

Deci & Ryan (2000) treat Higgins' Regulatory Focus as one research perspective on "goal-pursuit systems":
- Promotion/Prevention in Regulatory Focus corresponds to Approach/Avoidance goals in SDT
- Fear Appeal = Prevention-framed messages (confirmed by Tannenbaum et al., 2015)

## Relationship Between SDT and Self-Presentation

Lewis & Neighbors (2005):
- Autonomy (the core indicator in SDT) is **negatively** associated with Enhancement (an impression management strategy)
- → Individuals with higher autonomy are less likely to report health data in a socially desirable way
- However, SDT lacks coping appraisal, making it less comparable to Fear Appeal than Keller (2006)

## Scale Simplification Strategy (2026-03-10)

Pelletier et al. (1997) has 6 dimensions; the approach is to select the 1–2 items with the highest factor loading from each dimension.
